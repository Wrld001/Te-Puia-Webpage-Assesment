Te Puia's Environment

There are over 500 pools and 65 geyser vents, each with their own name, found in the 70-hectare Te Whakarewarewa geothermal valley in Rotorua. The largest geyser, Pohutu, erupts up to 20 times a day, reaching heights of 30 meters (100 feet).

The environment around Te Puia is rich with geothermal activity, unique flora, and wildlife. The boiling mud pools and geysers are not just a spectacular sight but also serve as a powerful reminder of the Earth's geothermal energy, which Māori have harnessed for cooking, bathing, and heating for centuries.
