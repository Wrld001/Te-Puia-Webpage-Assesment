Wood Carving Schools

National Carving School – Te Wānanga Whakairo. In 1967, the first intake to the National Carving School began learning the disciplines of their Māori ancestors.

The curriculum for this school is founded on centuries of knowledge and traditions, with an emphasis on tikanga (protocols) and kawa (customs). Learning the history of the art, understanding the significance of each piece, and developing the skills to create works that honor the past are fundamental components of the program.
